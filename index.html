Medical Emergency

Fall, seizure, chest pains, stroke

Action Protocol

  1. Assess the scene for any immediate dangers.
  2. Call 999 immediately, stating the situation and location.
  3. Provide first aid within your training. Do not move if a spinal injury is suspected.
  4. Keep the person calm and comfortable.
  5. Inform your line manager and follow incident reporting procedures.

Self-Harm

Person has actively harmed themselves

Action Protocol

  1. Ensure immediate safety. Remove items that could be used for further harm.
  2. Provide immediate first aid. If severe, call 999.
  3. Stay with the person. Speak calmly and non-judgementally.
  4. Report immediately to your line manager and safeguarding lead.
  5. Follow safeguarding protocol; this will likely involve referral to mental health or social services.

Suicidal Ideation

Person expressing thoughts of ending their life

Action Protocol

  1. Take all expressions seriously.
  2. Stay with the person and listen without judgment.
  3. If at immediate risk, call 999 or help them contact a crisis team/Samaritans (116 123).
  4. Report immediately to your line manager and safeguarding lead.
  5. Do not agree to secrecy. Explain you must get help to keep them safe.

Helplines & Resources

  • Samaritans: Call 116 123 (free, 24/7)
  • National Suicide Prevention Helpline: Call 0800 689 5652

Unexplained Injury or Suspected Neglect

Addressing concerns and documenting evidence

Action Protocol

  1. Address immediate first aid needs.
  2. Ask open, non-leading questions about the injury (e.g., "Can you tell me what happened?").
  3. Document everything you see and are told, using the person's exact words.
  4. Report concerns to your safeguarding lead immediately. Do not investigate yourself.

Refusal of Care or Medication

Handling situations where care is declined

Action Protocol

  1. Try to understand the reason for the refusal.
  2. Gently explain the importance of the care/medication.
  3. Check if the person has the mental capacity to make this decision.
  4. Never force care or medication.
  5. Document the refusal clearly and inform your line manager and relevant healthcare professional.

Missing Person

Responding when a person cannot be found

Action Protocol

  1. Confirm the person is missing by checking all usual locations.
  2. Gather key information: description, clothing, last seen time/place, medical needs.
  3. Inform your line manager immediately.
  4. Call 999 to report a vulnerable person is missing. Provide all gathered information.
  5. Secure the person's home or room as appropriate.
  6. Document all actions taken, conversations, and timings.

Unexpected Death

Protocol for finding a deceased person

Action Protocol

  1. Check for signs of life. If unsure, call 999 for emergency medical help.
  2. If the person is clearly deceased, do not disturb the scene.
  3. Call the police on 999 and inform them of an unexpected death.
  4. Contact your line manager immediately.
  5. Preserve the dignity of the deceased as much as possible without disturbing the scene.
  6. Document all observations and actions factually and chronologically.

Aggressive or Violent Behaviour

De-escalation and safety protocol

Action Protocol

  1. Prioritise your safety and the safety of others. Do not put yourself in harm's way.
  2. Attempt to de-escalate the situation verbally if safe. Use a calm and non-confrontational tone.
  3. Remove yourself, and others if possible, from the immediate area of danger.
  4. If there is an immediate risk of harm, call 999 and ask for the police.
  5. Report the incident to your line manager as soon as it is safe to do so.
  6. Document the incident in detail, including triggers, behaviour, and actions taken.
